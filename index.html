1

Get Started

Place your left hand on the home row keys (A, S, D, F) and your right hand on (G, H, J, K). These correspond to the white keys C through C of the next octave.

2

Play White Keys

Press A through K to play the natural notes (C, D, E, F, G, A, B, C). Try pressing them in order to hear the C major scale.

3

Add Sharps & Flats

Use the top row keys (W, E, T, Y, U) to play the black keys (sharps/flats). These sit between certain white keys.

4

Change Octaves

Use the octave controls above the piano to shift the pitch range up or down. Lower octaves produce deeper tones, higher octaves produce brighter ones.

5

Record & Playback

Click Record, play your melody, then click Record again to stop. Press Play to hear your creation. Use Clear to start fresh.

♫

Start Slow

Practice each exercise slowly at first. Speed will come naturally with muscle memory. Focus on accuracy over speed.

♪

Listen Carefully

Pay attention to how each note sounds. Try to recognize intervals—the distance between two notes. This builds your ear for music.

♩

Use the Recording Feature

Record yourself playing and listen back. It helps you identify mistakes and track your progress over time.

♯

Explore Octaves

Try playing the same melody in different octaves. Notice how the character of the music changes with higher or lower pitches.

𝄞

Practice Daily

Even 10 minutes of daily practice is better than an hour once a week. Consistency builds lasting musical skill.

🎹

Experiment Freely

Don't just follow exercises. Try playing random keys, create your own melodies, and have fun. Creativity is the heart of music.
